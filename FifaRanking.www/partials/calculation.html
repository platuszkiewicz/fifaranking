What is FIFA ranking?

FIFA World Ranking was introduced in December 1992. The main purpose of this ranking is to represent the force of each country in world football competition. Ranking is based on national team results in last four years. Also it is used to determine the seeds for the World Cup Finals and World Cup Qualifications. For example, before European part of World Cup Qualifications it is common to divide teams: 'first' nine teams go to bracket 1., second 'nine' teams go to bracket 2., and so on. Then comes the draw, and in Qualifications every group has one team from a bracket 1., one from bracket 2., and so on. In this case FIFA ranking helps to provide possibly equal qualification groups.

Current calculation method

Current calculation method was introduced after 2006 FIFA World Cup, but it had some minor improvements by that time. For ranking are taken matches played by first, representative team (the "A" team). This means, that there are taken into account following matches:

  • FIFA World Cup
  • FIFA World Cup qualifiers
  • FIFA Confederations Cup
  • continental final tournaments (i.e. UEFA EURO)
  • continental qualifying competitions (i.e. qualifications to UEFA EURO)
  • and international friendlies
For each match the national team gets some points. The number of points depends on following factors:
  • Match result
  • Match status
  • Opposition strength
  • Regional strength
Each factor is calculated into number, and then points given to team for a match can be calculated from formula:

Match result

This factor is the simplest one and depends on result:

  • 3 points - win (no penalty)
  • 2 points - win (penalty shootout)
  • 1 point - draw
  • 1 point - loss (penalty shootout)
  • 0 points - loss (no penalty shootout)

Match status

FIFA tried to differ matches of not equal importance. There is no doubt that matches on World Cup have higher significance than friendly matches, when it is common to play with young or less experienced players.

  • 4.0 point - World Cup finals match
  • 3.0 point - Continental cup (i.e. UEFA EURO) and Confederations Cup finals
  • 2.5 point - Qualifiers for FIFA World Cup and Continental cup
  • 1.0 point - Friendly match

Final calculation

To get ranking points of some team, there are taken into account all its matches from last 4 years (48 months). For each year there is calculated average for team per match. Then, these averages are summed with weighting:

  • 1.0 - matches within last 12 months
  • 0.5 - 12–24 months ago
  • 0.3 - 24–36 months ago
  • 0.2 - 36–48 months ago

Example (single match)

On 11th November 2016 in Bucharest, Poland won 3-0 with Romania in World Cup 2018 Qualifiers. For this match Poland will have 1479 points, because:

  • 3 in "Match result" (won)
  • 2.5 in "Match status" (Qualifiers for FIFA World Cup)
  • 1.66 in "Oppostion strength" (Romania were ranked at #34 position)
  • 0.99 in "Regional strength" (both teams are part of UEFA confederation)
If we multiply these numbers, and multiply result by 100 we get 1479 points for Poland for this match. To see more examples, please visit Wikipiedia.

To check whether I understand FIFA method of calculation ranking, I decided to analyze Poland national team matches from years 2013-2016 and compare result with last ranking in 2016. This means, that matches from "last 12 months" are all matches in 2016, matches from "1-2 years ago" are matches in 2015 and so on. I created spredsheet, copied results of matches from soccerstand.com and applied calculation method showed in this article. I received same numbers as at FIFA website, which is a good thing, but I discovered some differences. I am not sure whether I made a mistake or FIFA did (users on football-rankings.info noticed that FIFA makes some minor mistakes in their calcualtion from time to time). However, differences are rather small and here comes a list:
